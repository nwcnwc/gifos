GifOS · Sign a GIF 🛡️

Prove you made an app GIF. Signing binds these exact app bytes to your domain or email, so GifOS shows “Signed by you” to anyone who opens it. Everything happens in your browser — no upload, no account. Your app's saved data is never part of the signature, so it stays valid as people use the app.

Signs with an Ed25519 key you generate here. Publish the public half at https://yourdomain/gifos.key and anyone can verify — the key location is derived from the domain, so “Signed by it” means you control it.

1. Get a signing key

Keep the private key file safe — it's how you sign. Losing it just means generating a new one and re-publishing gifos.key.

2. Sign the GIF

Signs with your own OpenPGP key using your existing tools — Ed25519 and RSA (2048+) keys both work, and your private key never touches this page. Others verify against the copy of your key on keys.openpgp.org, so publish your key there first and confirm your email.

1. Download the statement to sign

2. Sign it with your PGP key (in a terminal):

gpg --detach-sign --digest-algo SHA256 gifos-statement.bin

That makes gifos-statement.bin.sig.

3. Upload the .sig and download your signed GIF

Signing proves authorship, not safety. Anyone can strip a signature (the file just becomes anonymous), and a signature only says the contents are unchanged since signing. ← Back to GifOS
